Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to computer systems designed to perform tasks that normally
require human intelligence. These tasks include learning, reasoning, problem-solving, and
understanding language. AI systems are commonly classified into narrow AI, which is designed for
a specific task, and general AI, which aims to perform any intellectual task a human can do.
Machine learning is a major subset of AI. It focuses on enabling systems to learn from data rather
than relying on explicitly programmed rules. Popular machine learning approaches include
supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning. Large Language Models
(LLMs) are a recent advancement in AI. They are trained on vast amounts of text data and can
generate human-like responses, summarize documents, answer questions, and assist with
programming tasks. Due to their size and complexity, efficient evaluation and summarization testing
is important. This short document is intentionally concise and suitable for testing text summarization
models without exceeding free API usage limits.
